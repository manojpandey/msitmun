    Maharaj Surajmal Institute of Technology

    Maharaja Surajmal Institute of Technology is an AICTE approved Institute under GGSIP University and is located in the Institutional area of Janakpuri, New Delhi.
    Constantly ranked amongst the top engineering college in Delhi NCR, Maharaja Surajmal Institute’s campus comprises of well equipped fully air-conditioned laboratories and a well-stocked fully computerized library which provides an invigorating ambience and permits holistic development of students.

    Secretariat’s message

    "Dear Delegates,

    We are honoured in inviting you to the inaugural edition of Maharaja Surajmal Institute of Technology Model United Nations, to be held on the 13th and 14th of August 2016 at the MSIT Campus at MSITMUN ‘16. We aim to provide delegates with a challenging experience of negotiation and diplomacy by assuming the roles of UN representatives and members of other international decision-making bodies. Delegates from all over the country are invited to converge to confront and debate issues of international importance. MSITMUN expects to promote not just simple public speaking or a sense of international relations, but also skills such as diplomacy, public speaking, cooperation, negotiation, and compromise. A variety of committees and agendas will ensure engaging and thought-provoking experience for each and every delegate, ranging from the European Club Association to the United Nations General Assembly to address some of the most important issues of our time."

    Whether you have attended a Model United Nations Conference before or considering participating for the first time, we strongly encourage you to attend the conference to experience how Debate and Knowledge Sharing help in finding solutions for contemporary pressing issues, moreover how the aforementioned qualities bring change in how we think and look at those issues. MSITMUN ‘16 is an exciting opportunity to showcase your debating and negotiating skills, and we hope that you will choose to take part in it.

    Quoting Desmond Tutu, "Don't raise your voice, improve your argument”, we must collectively think of improving our ability to reason and argue, and to put up a better fight for the cause
